Career Plan

When I read the book The Old Man and The Sea in my childhood for the first time, one sentence impressed me and always pops up in my mind since then. It says, my big fish must be somewhere. After I went to university and majored in finance, I gradually found that might have previewed my future career when I look at all the red and green numbers flashing on the screen. From my point of view, a great investor, similar to a great fisherman, is driven by courage, perseverance, and endurance. Dreaming of becoming such an expert, I hope to discover the law of fluctuation in the sea of global financial market as a portfolio manager, enabling more amateur investors to catch their own big fish.

In the short term, I want to continue learning and honing my craft in top investment banks or funds as an research analyst. Through this experience, I hope to gain sector-wide perspectives and develop a mature attitude towards deviation from expectations. To be a competitive candidate, not only will I devote myself to my studies at London Business School to advance my expertise and skills, but I will also seek opportunities to develop precise judgement and professional maturity. I believe that the MFA Programme will lay me a solid foundation for my career, which I already felt from our rigorous curriculums >_< and career development training.

After becoming an exceptional analyst, my goal is to build my own stock portfolio as a fund manager. To prepare myself, I will gather insights of research, analysis and valuation during my role as a research analyst. Additionally, a comprehensive understanding of main industries will allow me to make keen investment decisions. I will also establish connections with industry partners from various fields, including alumni of LBS, to exchange ideas with and benefit from their unique views about economics and markets.
